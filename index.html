About Me

Hi, and thank you for visiting my profile. I’m Muhammad Imran, a researcher in Software Engineering currently based in Italy. I am completing my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the University of L’Aquila, where I work at the SPENCER Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Vittorio Cortellessa.

My research focuses on automating performance problem detection in software systems using empirical methods, machine learning, and large language models (LLMs). I explore the relationship between source code features and performance behavior, and have worked on performance test coverage analysis, energy modeling in Java, and LLM-based performance bug detection. My work integrates static code analysis, dynamic analysis unsing profiling tools, and predictive modeling, with publications to venues such as EMSE and EASE.

In addition to research, I have over seven years of university-level teaching experience and enjoy simplifying complex technical concepts for higher education students. I’m especially interested in bridging theory and practice, and supporting the development of tools that help developers build efficient and reliable software.

Feel free to explore my work or reach out for collaboration!
